About Me

Hi! I am Ares Koumblis (Greek: Άρης Κουμπλής), a Senior Autopilot Software Engineer at Tesla, working on the evaluation and reinforcement learning fine-tuning of end-to-end autonomous driving models. I hold a BE/ME in Electrical and Computer Engineering from the National Technical University of Athens, with a concentration in Computer Science. While there, I founded and led the Formula Student (FSAE) autonomous driving team within PROM Racing NTUA. My thesis focused on the autonomous driving pipeline deployed on the team's first driverless car, advised by Prof. Constantinos Tzafestas.

My experience, from engineering a camera-only autonomous stack for an FSAE race car to adapting and fine-tuning control policies for Tesla’s production vehicles, has been shaped by confronting the limits of autonomy at scale. I learned that reliance on manually engineered logic and heuristics makes systems brittle in unpredictable environments.

These experiences drive my interest in scalable robot learning. I aim to develop generalist agents that can adapt quickly to new tasks and operate safely in previously unseen conditions. I am particularly interested in learning representations and algorithms that unify perception, control, and reasoning, moving beyond data imitation to create adaptive, interpretable, and robust autonomous systems.
